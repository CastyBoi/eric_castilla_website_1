Photography Journey

I like to take pictures, and there's something about the art of composition and capturing a moment that does the soul good.

The first camera I used was a Sony A6000. This was a great entry level place to start, and there was a plethora of lenses available for various scenarios. I used this for several years and was able to capture trips to San Fransisco, Carmel Beach, and Sedona. I even got to take some graduation pictures for family and friends.

I eventually upgraded to a Sony A7ii, and I LOVED the photos it was able to produce. The Field of View was much greater, and my lightroom skills slowly got better. A pretty big upgrade but really expensive to fully utilize. Full frame lenses get expensive, and I just didn't use this one a ton.

After selling the A7ii, I was gifted a Panasonic Lumix G100. I still have this one, and it has proven itself to be very easy to carry and use. The more compact size really lends itself to staying on your person, and like they say "the best camera is the one you have with you" or whatever.

After this I tried using a pretty old DSLR, the Canon 5D MKii. Its an oldie but a goldie, and the full frame sensor lets me get back that Field of View I am missing in the Lumix. This was fun shooting with cheap and kinda shitty lenses, but you miss the features of a new camera after a while.

My Fianceé (after getting her big girl job as an RN, SO proud of her) bought me a Canon RP and I picked up a 35mm lens. After using the 5D for a while I liked the ergo and color reproduction so it made sense to get into a newer full frame. Loving this one, some really awesome pictures have come from me using this one more regularly.

As hinted at in many of my images, I like cars. I've owned and driven several over the past few years. Notable mentions include: a Miata, an EK Civic Hatchback, a Subaru BRZ, a Subaru WRX, another Miata, and most recently a Volkswagen GTI. It's been fun figuring out what I like, and I think the GTI is the perfect complement to more "Hardcore" cars being either more offroad built cars or track built sports cars.

I do light mods and repairs ranging from exhaust installs to full suspension upgrades and general engine repairs. It's my goal one day to properly build an engine and turbo a non-turbo car, but that will hopefully come when I finally buy a house at age 50.
